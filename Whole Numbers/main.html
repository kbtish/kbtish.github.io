Wednesday 4/20

Today we started looking at subtraction. Some important things to remember are:
  • The order of the numbers is important ("3 minus 1" is NOT the same as "1 minus 3")
  • If the bottom digit is bigger than the top digit, we need to borrow/regroup
  • We can check our answer by adding
    (For example, 34 - 15 = 19 ... Check the answer by adding ... 15 + 19 = 34)

Wednesday 4/13

We started looking at addition word problems.

Use addition if the word problem involves:
  • combining things
  • putting things together
  • things that can be counted together

Watch out for extra information that is not needed to answer the question. (These are sometimes called "red herrings".)
For example, a problem might tell you about apples and oranges, but only ask about apples.
